---
title: "Call Recording Consent Rules by Region"
---

Call recording laws vary significantly by jurisdiction. The core question in every region is the same: do you need the consent of one party to the call, or all parties?

[RapidCall.ai](http://RapidCall.ai) provides the technical capability to record calls. Whether recording is enabled, and whether required consent has been obtained, is entirely your responsibility. The platform does not enforce consent collection — it is a tool, and how you use it must comply with the laws of the jurisdictions where your contacts are located.

Here is a practical overview of the major frameworks:

### **United States**

The US has a patchwork of federal and state laws. At the federal level, the Wiretap Act requires one-party consent — meaning at least one party to the call must consent to the recording. Since your AI agent is acting on your behalf, your authorisation typically satisfies this requirement.

However, several states require all-party consent, meaning every person on the call must be informed and agree to the recording. The states with all-party (or two-party) consent laws include California, Connecticut, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, New Hampshire, Oregon, Pennsylvania, and Washington. If your contacts are in any of these states, you must disclose recording at the start of the call.

| ⚠️ Practical recommendation: Regardless of which states you are calling, include a recording disclosure in your agent’s opening script. It takes a few seconds, it satisfies all-party consent requirements everywhere, and it eliminates the need to maintain state-by-state logic. The simplest approach: have the agent say “this call may be recorded for quality purposes” before the conversation begins. |

Additional US regulations to be aware of:

| **Regulation** | **What It Requires** |
| :-- | :-- |
| TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) | Prior express consent for autodialled or prerecorded calls to mobile phones. Prior express written consent for telemarketing calls. Maintain an internal Do-Not-Call list. Honour the National DNC Registry. |
| FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule | Restrictions on calling hours (8am–9pm local time). Caller identification requirements. Prohibition on deceptive practices. |
| State-level telemarketing laws | Many states have additional registration, licensing, or disclosure requirements for telemarketing. Check the laws of every state you are calling into. |

### **European Union / EEA / United Kingdom**

The GDPR applies to any processing of personal data of individuals in the EU/EEA. Call recordings and transcripts that contain personal data (names, phone numbers, account details) are personal data under the GDPR.

In addition to the GDPR, the ePrivacy Directive (and its national implementations) governs electronic communications, including automated calls. Most EU member states require consent for automated outbound calls to individuals, particularly for marketing purposes. The UK’s Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) impose similar requirements.

For call recording specifically, most EU/EEA jurisdictions require that the caller informs the recipient that the call is being recorded and provides a valid legal basis. In practice, for business-to-customer calls, this means either explicit consent or a demonstrable legitimate interest with appropriate safeguards.

| 📋 Practical recommendation: For EU/EEA operations, include both an AI disclosure and a recording disclosure in the agent’s script. Maintain records of the legal basis for each call campaign. Ensure your privacy notice covers AI-assisted communications and call recording. |
| :-- |

### **Other Regions**

Australia’s Telecommunications (Interception and Access) Act requires one-party consent at the federal level, though state laws vary. Canada’s PIPEDA and CASL impose consent requirements for both recording and commercial electronic messages, including calls. The Middle East, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America have varying frameworks — if you are operating in these regions, consult a local legal advisor.

The general principle across all regions: when in doubt, disclose. Telling people the call is recorded and AI-powered costs you a few seconds per call. Not telling them can cost you significantly more.
