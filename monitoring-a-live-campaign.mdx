---
title: "Monitoring a Live Campaign"
---

Once a campaign is running, you need visibility into what is happening in real time. The platform gives you two primary monitoring tools, and your contact list gives you a third.

**Call History. **Every call appears in Call History as it completes. Filter by agent, date range, or phone number to see only the calls from your active campaign. Each row shows the outcome, duration, sentiment, and cost. Click any call to open the detail panel with the full transcript, recording, and extracted data. If something is going wrong — the agent is mishandling a step, a function is not firing, contacts are hanging up at the same point — the transcripts will show you exactly where.

**Performance dashboard. **The Performance page gives you aggregate metrics across your campaign: total calls, answer rate, average duration, transfer rate, voicemail rate, cost per minute, and sentiment distribution. The By Agent view shows these metrics per agent, which is useful if you are running multiple agents or A/B testing different prompts. Section 5 covers the analytics dashboard in detail.

**Your contact list. **If your workflow is writing call outcomes back to the contact list (which it should be), the list itself becomes a real-time campaign dashboard. Sort or filter by status to see how many contacts have been reached, how many are pending retry, how many declined, and how many converted. For managers who do not need the full platform dashboard, the contact list is often the most accessible view of campaign progress.

Check your campaign at least once in the first 30 minutes after launch. Listen to two or three recordings, read the transcripts, and confirm the agent is behaving the way you expect. Most issues surface in the first dozen calls. Catching them early saves you from running a broken agent for hours.
