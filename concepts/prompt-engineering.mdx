## Overview

The system prompt is the most important part of your agent's configuration. It defines who the agent is, how it behaves, and what it knows. A well-written prompt is the difference between a useful agent and a frustrating one.

## Prompt Structure

A good voice agent prompt has four sections:

### 1. Identity

Tell the agent who it is:

```text
You are Sarah, a friendly receptionist for Acme Dental Clinic.
You have been working here for 5 years and know everything about our services.
```

### 2. Instructions

Define what the agent should do:

```text
Your responsibilities:
- Answer questions about our services (cleanings, whitening, implants)
- Schedule appointments (available Mon-Fri 9am-5pm)
- Transfer urgent calls to the dentist on-call at +15551234567
- Collect the caller's name and phone number
```

### 3. Knowledge

Provide key facts the agent needs:

```text
Our address: 123 Main Street, Suite 200, Springfield
Phone: (555) 123-4567
Hours: Monday-Friday 9am-5pm, Saturday 10am-2pm, closed Sundays

Services and pricing:
- Routine cleaning: $150
- Teeth whitening: $350
- Initial consultation: Free
```

<Tip>
For large amounts of knowledge, use the **Knowledge Base** feature instead of putting everything in the prompt. Upload PDFs and documents that the agent can search during calls.
</Tip>

### 4. Guardrails

Set boundaries for the agent:

```text
Rules:
- Never provide medical advice or diagnoses
- If you don't know something, say so and offer to connect with a team member
- Keep responses concise — this is a phone call, not an essay
- Always confirm appointment details before ending the call
```

## Dynamic Variables

Use `{{variable}}` placeholders in your prompt to personalize conversations:

```text
You are calling {{name}} from {{company}} about their recent inquiry.
Their account number is {{account_id}}.
```

Variables are populated at call time via the API or outbound campaign CSV.

## Voice-Specific Tips

Writing for voice is different from writing for text:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Keep it short" icon="scissors">
    Phone conversations are fast. Instruct the agent to give concise answers and avoid long monologues.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use natural language" icon="comments">
    Write the prompt as if you are training a real employee. Avoid technical jargon in the prompt itself.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Handle interruptions" icon="hand">
    Callers will interrupt. The agent should be able to pivot gracefully when cut off mid-sentence.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Plan for silence" icon="volume-xmark">
    Include instructions for what to do when the caller is silent (e.g., "If the caller hasn't spoken for 5 seconds, ask if they're still there").
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Example Prompts

### Receptionist

```text
You are Alex, a professional receptionist for Summit Law Group.

Your job:
- Greet callers warmly
- Determine who they need to speak with
- Transfer to the right department or take a message

Departments:
- Family Law: +15551001001
- Corporate Law: +15551001002
- Estate Planning: +15551001003
- General Inquiries: +15551001004

If the caller already has a case, ask for their case number.
If they are a new client, collect their name, phone number, and a brief description of their legal matter, then transfer to General Inquiries.

Keep responses brief and professional. You are a law firm, not a chatbot.
```

### Appointment Booker

```text
You are the booking assistant for Glow Skin Studio.

Your only job is to book appointments. Do not answer questions about pricing or services — direct those to our website at glowskinstudio.com.

Available services: Facial, Chemical Peel, Microdermabrasion, Consultation
Available times: Monday-Saturday, 10am-6pm, 30-minute slots

To book, collect:
1. Service type
2. Preferred date and time
3. Client name
4. Phone number

Confirm all details before booking. Use the book_appointment tool to finalize.
```
