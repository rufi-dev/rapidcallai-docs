---
title: "Call Scheduling & Timing"
---

When you place a call matters almost as much as what the agent says.

Even a strong agent will underperform if it is calling people at the wrong time. Good scheduling improves answer rates, protects compliance, and increases the chances of reaching contacts when they are actually available to engage.

### Respect local time zones

If your contacts are spread across different regions, your workflow needs to account for local time.

A call placed at 9:00 AM in your own time zone may reach the contact far too early or too late in theirs. Your scheduling logic should always use the contact’s local time when deciding when to call.

Most workflow tools, including Make.com and n8n, support timezone-aware scheduling. Use it.

### Respect legal calling hours

Your campaign setup is responsible for enforcing legal calling windows.

RapidCall will place calls whenever your workflow triggers them, so it is up to your scheduling logic to make sure calls are only made during permitted hours in the relevant jurisdiction.

For example, in the United States, the FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule generally limits telemarketing calls to **8:00 AM to 9:00 PM local time**. Other countries and regions may have different rules.

Always make sure your workflows follow the applicable laws for the markets you are calling.

### Front-load your calling window

In most outbound campaigns, answer rates are strongest earlier in the day.

As a general pattern:

- **9:00 AM to 11:00 AM local time** often performs well,
- **1:00 PM to 3:00 PM local time** is usually another strong window,
- calls after **5:00 PM** can still work, especially for consumer-facing campaigns, but performance is often less consistent.

If you have a limited calling window, begin with the morning period first.

### Use multiple calling windows

Do not rely on a single pass through the list.

A contact who does not answer in the morning may be available later in the day. Splitting outreach across two or three calling windows usually improves total reach without requiring major changes to the workflow.

A common structure is:

- morning window,
- midday window,
- afternoon window.

As contacts are reached, your workflow should exclude them from later passes automatically.

### Prioritise speed-to-contact for new leads

If your contact list is fed by live lead sources such as website forms, inbound enquiries, or ad submissions, response speed matters more than almost anything else.

New leads are most valuable when contacted quickly. The first call should ideally be triggered as soon as the lead arrives in your system.

If the use case depends on fresh intent, build your workflow so the initial call happens immediately rather than waiting for the next scheduled batch.

### Best practice

A strong outbound schedule should:

- use the contact’s local time zone,
- stay within legal calling hours,
- prioritise the highest-performing windows,
- spread retries across multiple periods,
- and move quickly when new leads arrive.

Well-timed calls do not just improve answer rates. They improve the entire campaign.
