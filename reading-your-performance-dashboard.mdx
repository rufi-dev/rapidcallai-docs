---
title: "Reading Your Performance Dashboard"
---

The Performance section sits under Monitor in the left sidebar. It is the first place you should look every morning.

The dashboard has two views: General (all agents combined) and By Agent (per-agent breakdown). A date filter at the top right lets you narrow the data to any time range — today, last 7 days, this month, or a custom window. 

### **Top-Line Metrics**

The first thing you see is a row of headline numbers across the top. Each one tells you something specific about how your agents are performing at a glance.

**Total Calls** — the total number of calls placed or received in the selected period by all agents in your workspace. This is your volume indicator. If this number is lower than expected, check whether your campaigns are running, whether phone numbers are correctly assigned, and whether your contact lists are populated.

**Successful Calls** — the number of calls where the contact / client actually answered and the agent had a conversation. 

**Answer Rate** — the percentage of calls that were picked up. This is the clearest indicator of list quality and calling hygiene. Low answer rates point to stale contact data, bad calling windows, or caller ID issues. For most outbound campaigns, anything above 50% is healthy. Below 50% means something in the setup needs fixing before you worry about the script. If you are seeing answer rates below 50%, look at your calling hours, caller ID configuration, and whether your number is getting flagged as spam.

**Unique Numbers** — how many unique phone numbers / clients your agents called during the period. 

**Avg. Duration** — the average length of connected calls. This one is context-dependent. For appointment setting or lead qualification, 45 seconds to 2 minutes is typical. If your average is under 30 seconds, contacts are likely hanging up early — the opening line or the voice may need work. If it is over 3 minutes, the agent might be rambling or getting stuck in loops. Compare this against your best-performing agent to calibrate.

### **Operational Metrics**

Below the top line, a second row gives you the operational detail.

**Transfer Rate** — the percentage of calls that were escalated to a human. Some transfer is expected and healthy — it means the agent is routing complex conversations correctly. If transfer rate is high (above 20–25%), the agent is likely hitting edge cases it does not know how to handle, and the prompt or handlers need tightening. If it is near zero, check whether your transfer functions are actually wired up and firing.

**Voicemail Rate** — the percentage of calls where the agent reached voicemail. If you have voicemail detection enabled and configured to hang up, this should be very low. If it is high, your detection settings may need adjustment, or you are calling at times when people are unlikely to answer.

**Avg. Calls/Day** — your daily call volume averaged across the period. Use this to spot trends. A sudden drop means a campaign stopped or a number went down. This is also the number you use when forecasting costs.

**Avg. Cost/Min** — your blended cost per minute of talk time across all agents and voice stacks. This combines platform fees, LLM inference, and voice provider charges. Use it to compare agents running different voice stacks, or to track whether a configuration change improved cost efficiency. If you are running Claude Sonnet \+ Cartesia on one agent and Gemini Studio on another, this metric will show you the real-world cost difference.

### **Call Volume Chart**

Below the metric cards, a bar chart shows total calls by day across the selected period, with the cumulative total in the top right corner. This is the fastest way to spot patterns: which days of the week produce the most volume, whether there are unexpected gaps (campaigns paused, numbers down, telephony issues), and how volume is trending over time. If you are running multiple campaigns, switch to the By Agent view to see which agent is driving the volume. 

### **Customer Sentiment**

The platform automatically analyses every completed call transcript and classifies the contact’s sentiment as positive, neutral, or negative. The results are shown as a donut chart with exact counts and percentages.

Sentiment is not a vanity metric. It tells you how your contacts feel about the interaction, which is a leading indicator of conversion and brand risk. If 80%\+ of your calls are landing positive - neutral, the script and voice are doing their job. If negative sentiment starts climbing above 10%, something is off — the tone, the pitch, the timing of calls, or the way objections are being handled. Pull the transcripts from negative calls and read them. The problem is usually obvious once you see the actual conversation.

### **Outcomes**

The Outcomes chart breaks down how calls ended. This is where you diagnose conversion and operational issues.

**User Hangup** — the contact ended the call. This is the most common outcome for outbound and not necessarily bad. What matters is when they hung up. If contacts are dropping in the first 10 seconds, the opening line or voice is the problem. If they are dropping after 30–60 seconds, the pitch is not landing. Cross-reference with call duration and listen to a sample of these recordings.

**No Answer** — the call was not picked up. High no-answer rates are a list quality or calling window issue, not an agent issue.

**Transferred** — the agent routed the call to a human. Compare this against your Transfer Rate metric. 

**Agent Hangup** — the agent ended the call according to its script logic. This is the expected outcome when the conversation reaches a natural close — appointment booked, information delivered, contact declined politely. If this number is unusually low, the agent may not be reaching its closing step, which means the call flow has a gap.

**Other** — catches everything else: max duration reached, telephony errors, dropped connections. If this climbs above 5–10%, investigate. It usually points to a technical issue rather than a script problem.

| 💡 Tip: The most useful habit is to check the Performance dashboard each morning, skim the numbers, and then pull 3–5 random call recordings from the previous day. Numbers tell you what happened. Recordings tell you why. |
