---
title: "How AI agents works"
---

An AI voice agent is a software-based employee that answers or places phone calls, converses in natural language, and takes action based on how the conversation unfolds — without human involvement.

RapidCall supports two agent modes:

**Inbound** — the agent receives calls on an assigned phone number. The moment a caller connects, the AI picks up instantly, identifies the purpose of the call, and handles it end-to-end: answering questions, qualifying leads, booking appointments, escalating to a human, or any combination of flows you define.

**Outbound** — the agent works through a contact list you upload / connect or sync via CRM. It dials each number on a schedule you set, delivers its opening, and adapts the conversation in real time based on how the contact responds — handling objections, collecting data, or pushing toward the defined / desired outcome.

From your side, setup is the same either way. You configure the agent once — its voice, prompt, behaviour logic, and connected systems. After that, the platform handles execution.

Call audio travels through your telephony infrastructure. Response latency is sub-second. The agent runs concurrently across as many active calls as your account allows, around the clock, with no degradation in consistency.

When a call ends, the outcome is logged, transcripts are stored, CRM records update, and any downstream actions you've configured — emails, SMS, calendar bookings, webhook triggers — fire automatically.
