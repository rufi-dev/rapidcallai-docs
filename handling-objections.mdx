---
title: "Handling Objections"
---

Objections are not problems. They are buying signals with friction attached. The agent’s job is not to win an argument — it is to acknowledge the concern, explore what is behind it, and redirect toward the next step.

We use a simple framework: identify the concern (acknowledge what the contact just said), explore the impact (ask how it affects them or what would need to change), and provide resolution (position your next step as the solution). Keep responses concise. Do not argue. Stay aligned with the caller. Guide toward action.

The most common objection types are price concerns, lack of urgency (‘I’m not looking right now’), need for more information, and comparison with competitors. Write specific handler responses for each one. Generic objection handling is where agents fall apart on live calls.
