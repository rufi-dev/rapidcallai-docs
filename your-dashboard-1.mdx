title: "Your Dashboard"
---

The dashboard is the central control area for your RapidCall workspace.

Everything is organised through the left-hand sidebar and follows a simple structure: **Build, Deploy, Monitor, and System**.

### **Build**

#### Agents

The Agents section is where you create, manage, and review your AI agents.

Each agent card gives you a quick overview, including connected phone numbers, total call volume, and last activity. From here, you can also import agents from other platforms using JSON, export agents for backup or review, and access agent-level performance reports.

When you open an agent, you enter the full configuration area where you can define:

- prompt and behaviour,
- voice and model stack,
- call settings,
- knowledge base,
- functions and actions,
- webhooks,
- and security settings.

#### Knowledge Base

The Knowledge Base is the information library your agents use during calls.

You can upload documents such as PDF, DOC, or TXT files, or paste text directly into the platform. Once connected to an agent, that knowledge becomes available during conversations without needing to place everything inside the prompt itself.

This helps keep prompts cleaner while giving agents access to the information they need.

### **Deploy**

#### Phone Numbers

The Phone Numbers section is where you manage all numbers connected to your workspace.

For each number, you can configure:

- inbound routing, which determines which agent answers incoming calls,
- outbound routing, which determines which agent places calls from that number,
- allowed country permissions,
- and optional trust or verification settings.

Before going live, the **Check Config** tool lets you confirm that the number and routing are set up correctly.

#### Outbound Calls

The Outbound Calls section lets you trigger individual calls manually.

To place a call, select the agent, choose the number the call should come from, and enter the destination in E.164 format using `+[country code][number]`. You can also choose whether to enable call recording.

Below the dialler, the jobs table shows recent outbound call attempts along with their status and result.

### **Monitor**

#### Call History

Call History provides a complete log of calls across all agents in the workspace.

You can filter calls by date, agent, or phone number. Each row includes key call details such as time, origin, destination, status, recording availability, cost, direction, duration, and latency.

Clicking into a call opens the detail view, where you can access:

- a playable recording with download option,
- call metrics such as cost, latency, sentiment, and token usage,
- an auto-generated summary,
- a timestamped transcript,
- extracted data fields,
- and technical logs.

Common call statuses include:

- **User Hung Up** - the contact ended the call,
- **Agent Ended** - the agent completed the call flow and ended the conversation,
- **No Answer** - the call was not picked up,
- **Max Duration** - the call reached the maximum time limit configured for the agent.

#### Performance

The Performance section gives you analytics across your calling activity.

You can view data in two modes:

- **General** - performance across all agents combined,
- **By Agent** - a breakdown for individual agents.

Metrics include:

- total calls,
- answer rate,
- average duration,
- transfer rate,
- voicemail rate,
- cost per minute,
- and customer sentiment.

Call volume is also displayed in a daily chart so you can track activity over time.

### **System**

#### Billing

The Billing section gives you a real-time view of usage and payment history.

The **Usage** tab breaks down costs by billing component, including:

- platform minutes,
- voice provider usage,
- and language model usage.

The **Invoices** area stores past invoices and allows you to download them when needed.

#### Settings

The Settings section controls workspace-wide configuration.

This includes:

- workspace identity,
- team access,
- API keys,
- webhooks,
- and usage limits.

You can invite team members with role-based access such as **Owner** or **Member**, generate API keys for custom integrations, and set spend or call-volume limits to keep usage under control.

### **Workspace Switcher**

At the top of the sidebar, the Workspace Switcher lets you move between fully isolated workspaces.

Each workspace has its own agents, phone numbers, billing, and team settings. This is especially useful for agencies or multi-brand businesses that need to keep environments completely separated.
